Sunday · June 14

Poached Salmon & Broccoli Sprouts

1
EPA / DHA Sulforaphane

Ingredients

  • 6 oz wild salmon fillet (skin-on)
  • 1 cup broccoli sprouts
  • ½ lemon, sliced
  • 2 cups water or light vegetable broth
  • 3–4 whole peppercorns
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Sea salt

Preparation

  1. Bring water or broth to a bare simmer with lemon, peppercorns, bay leaf, and salt.
  2. Slide salmon in and poach 8–10 minutes at low temperature.
  3. Rest briefly on a plate.
  4. Top with raw broccoli sprouts and a squeeze of lemon.

Monday · June 15

Avocado, Cucumber & Herb Bowl

2
Vitamin E Carotenoids Apigenin Silica

Ingredients

  • 1 ripe avocado, cubed
  • 1 medium cucumber, sliced
  • ¼ cup parsley, torn
  • ¼ cup basil leaves, torn
  • 2 tbsp mint, torn
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Sea salt and black pepper

Preparation

  1. Add avocado and cucumber to a bowl.
  2. Tear herbs over the top.
  3. Add lemon juice and toss gently.
  4. Season with salt and pepper and serve.

Tuesday · June 16

Kiwi, Chia & Hemp Yogurt Bowl

3
Vitamin C ALA Omega‑3 Fiber Low‑BCAA

Ingredients

  • ¾ cup plain hemp yogurt
  • 2 ripe kiwis, peeled and sliced
  • 1½ tbsp chia seeds
  • 1 tbsp hemp hearts
  • Pinch of ground cardamom
  • 1 tsp raw honey (optional)

Preparation

  1. Spoon hemp yogurt into a bowl.
  2. Sprinkle chia seeds over the top and let sit 3–5 minutes.
  3. Add sliced kiwi, hemp hearts, and cardamom.
  4. Drizzle with honey if using and serve promptly.

Wednesday · June 17

Electrolyte‑Supported Fasting Day

4
Sodium Potassium Magnesium Autophagy‑supportive

Electrolyte protocol

  • Water with a pinch of sea salt
  • Mineral water rich in electrolytes
  • Unsweetened herbal tea
  • 200–300 mg magnesium glycinate (optional)

Guidelines

  1. Hydrate steadily with water and sea salt.
  2. Include mineral water to support potassium intake.
  3. Use herbal teas as desired without sweeteners.
  4. Take magnesium later in the day if tolerated.
  5. Keep the day calorie‑free and break the fast gently the next day.

Thursday · June 18

Egg-White, Spinach & Mushroom Scramble

5
Ergothioneine Lutein Low‑BCAA

Ingredients

  • 4 large egg whites
  • 1 cup shiitake or oyster mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 big handfuls baby spinach
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp avocado oil
  • Sea salt, black pepper, fresh thyme

Preparation

  1. Set a pan over low‑medium heat.
  2. Add avocado oil, then garlic and mushrooms; sauté 4–5 minutes.
  3. Add spinach and cook until just wilted.
  4. Pour in egg whites and fold gently until just set and glossy.
  5. Season with salt, pepper, and thyme and serve.

Supplement Rhythm for a Single Daily Meal

This stack assumes one main meal later in the day, with a long fasting window. It pairs each supplement with the meal composition and fasting periods to support mitochondrial function, glycation control, autophagy, and antioxidant balance. Always review supplements with a clinician before starting or combining them.

Sunday · Poached Salmon & Broccoli Sprouts

Omega‑rich, anti‑inflammatory meal

Early waking window

  • Electrolytes: water with minerals to support hydration before the feeding window.
  • ALA (alpha‑lipoic acid): taken away from food to support insulin sensitivity and glycation control.

With main meal

  • Omega‑3 (EPA/DHA): complements salmon intake and supports lipid and brain health.
  • Astaxanthin: fat‑soluble antioxidant that pairs well with a higher‑fat fish meal.
  • Multivitamin: broad micronutrient coverage alongside a nutrient‑dense meal.
  • CoQ10: taken with fat to support mitochondrial electron transport.

Late feeding window

  • GlyNAC (glycine + NAC): supports glutathione production and redox balance after the meal.

Monday · Avocado, Cucumber & Herb Bowl

Monounsaturated‑rich, polyphenol‑heavy meal

With main meal

  • Astaxanthin: pairs with avocado fat to enhance absorption and antioxidant coverage.
  • Milk thistle: gentle liver support alongside herb‑rich, plant‑based intake.

Late feeding window

  • Spermidine: aligns with a lower‑protein, plant‑heavy day to support autophagy‑related pathways.

Wednesday · Electrolyte‑Supported Fasting

Deep fasting and autophagy emphasis

Away from any calories

Later in the day

With main meal

  • CoQ10: taken with the small amount of fat in the meal to support mitochondrial function.
  • Milk thistle: pairs with mushroom‑rich intake and hepatic detox pathways.

Late feeding window

  • GlyNAC: supports glutathione and redox balance after protein intake.
  • Additional magnesium or glycine: can be used to support relaxation and collagen synthesis.
