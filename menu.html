Not medical advice. This is an informational, personal-use meal and supplement reference — not a clinical nutrition or treatment plan. Talk to a doctor before starting or changing any supplement routine, particularly NAD+ precursors, anything affecting glucose metabolism, or long-term high-dose mineral use.

Carbs are kept generally low across the week but aren't gram-counted — a couple of days carry a fuller piece of fruit specifically because the compound it brings (quercetin, fisetin, PQQ, ellagic acid) has solid research behind it, not because the day needed more carbs. Sodium and saturated fat were both actively reduced in this plan — no cheese, no butter, low- or no-added-salt broth, and poaching instead of baking or searing on the fish day. "Low-BCAA" reflects a personal preference in how protein was chosen (gelatin from a slow braise, fish, and modest collagen top-ups, with several days built entirely around vegetables and no animal protein at all) — it is not a clinical BCAA-restriction protocol.
