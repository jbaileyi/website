Sunday · June 14

Poached Salmon &
Broccoli Sprouts

Gentle poaching keeps AGEs low while preserving omega‑3s and delicate proteins.

1
EPA / DHA Sulforaphane

Ingredients

  • 6 oz wild salmon fillet (skin-on for fat)
  • 1 cup fresh broccoli sprouts
  • ½ lemon, sliced
  • 2 cups water or light vegetable broth
  • 3–4 whole peppercorns
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Pinch of sea salt

Preparation

  1. Bring water or broth to a bare simmer (170–180°F) in a shallow pan with lemon, peppercorns, bay leaf, and salt.
  2. Slide salmon in skin-side down. Poach 8–10 min until flesh flakes easily — do not boil.
  3. Lift salmon out gently and rest 2 min on a plate.
  4. Top with raw broccoli sprouts and a squeeze of fresh lemon. Serve immediately.

Monday · June 15

Avocado, Cucumber
& Herb Bowl

Raw herbs, vitamin E, and carotenoids in a zero‑cook, low‑stress meal.

2
Vitamin E Carotenoids Apigenin Silica
Zero cooking required

Ingredients

  • 1 ripe avocado, cubed
  • 1 medium cucumber, sliced
  • ¼ cup fresh flat-leaf parsley
  • ¼ cup fresh basil leaves
  • 2 tbsp fresh mint
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Flaky sea salt & black pepper

Preparation

  1. Cube avocado and slice cucumber into half-moons. Place in a wide bowl.
  2. Roughly tear parsley, basil, and mint directly over the bowl — tearing releases more polyphenols than chopping.
  3. Squeeze lemon juice over everything and toss gently without mashing the avocado.
  4. Season with flaky salt and cracked pepper. Eat immediately to preserve vitamin E.

Tuesday · June 16

Kiwi, Chia &
Hemp Yogurt Bowl

Vitamin C, fiber, and ALA‑rich seeds in a low‑BCAA, gut‑friendly bowl.

3
Vitamin C ALA Omega-3 Fiber Low-BCAA

Ingredients

  • ¾ cup plain hemp yogurt (unsweetened)
  • 2 ripe kiwis, peeled & sliced
  • 1½ tbsp chia seeds
  • 1 tbsp hemp hearts
  • 1 tsp raw honey (optional)
  • Pinch of ground cardamom

Preparation

  1. Spoon hemp yogurt into a bowl as the base.
  2. Sprinkle chia seeds over yogurt and let sit 3–5 min to begin softening.
  3. Arrange sliced kiwi on top. Scatter hemp hearts and a pinch of cardamom.
  4. Drizzle with honey if desired. Serve right away — kiwi's vitamin C degrades quickly once cut.

Wednesday · June 17

Electrolyte‑Supported
Fasting Day

A structured fast to lean into autophagy while protecting volume status and minerals.

4
Sodium Potassium Magnesium Autophagy‑supportive

Electrolyte protocol

  • 24–36 oz water in the morning
  • ½ tsp sea salt dissolved in warm water
  • 200–300 mg magnesium glycinate (optional, evening)
  • 1 cup mineral water with natural potassium
  • Herbal tea (unsweetened)

Guidelines

  1. Begin the day with water + sea salt to maintain plasma volume.
  2. Drink mineral water throughout the day to support potassium balance.
  3. Use magnesium in the afternoon or evening to support muscle relaxation and sleep.
  4. Stick to non‑caloric beverages only — no sweeteners.
  5. Break the fast gently the next morning with fruit or warm broth.

Thursday · June 18

Egg-White, Spinach
& Mushroom Scramble

Low‑BCAA protein with ergothioneine‑rich mushrooms and lutein‑dense greens.

5
Ergothioneine Lutein Low-BCAA

Ingredients

  • 4 large egg whites
  • 1 cup shiitake or oyster mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 big handfuls baby spinach
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp avocado oil
  • Sea salt, black pepper, fresh thyme

Preparation

  1. Set pan to low-medium heat (≤250°F). High heat creates AGEs — keep it gentle.
  2. Add avocado oil, then garlic and mushrooms. Sauté 4–5 min until just tender.
  3. Add spinach and toss until wilted, about 1 min.
  4. Pour egg whites over vegetables. Fold slowly with a silicone spatula. Remove from heat when just set and still slightly glossy. Season with salt, pepper, and thyme.

Longevity‑Focused Supplement Rhythm

These are optional, food‑complementary supplements that pair with this plan’s focus on mitochondrial function, glycation control, and cellular repair. Always clear new supplements with your clinician, especially if you take medications or have liver, kidney, or cardiovascular conditions.

Morning · with or after first meal

Foundational & Mitochondrial Support

  • Multivitamin: covers baseline micronutrient gaps that food may not fully meet.
  • Omega‑3 (EPA/DHA): complements salmon day and supports lipids, brain, and inflammation tone.
  • CoQ10: supports mitochondrial electron transport, especially useful if on statins.
  • Astaxanthin: fat‑soluble antioxidant that pairs well with higher‑fat meals (salmon, avocado).

Late morning / Midday

Metabolic & Glycation Support

  • ALA (Alpha‑lipoic acid): supports insulin sensitivity and helps counter glycation.
  • GlyNAC (glycine + NAC): precursors for glutathione, supporting redox balance and detox pathways.
  • Milk thistle: gentle liver support, especially relevant with higher xenobiotic load or medications.

Afternoon · away from large meals

Cellular Renewal & Autophagy‑Aligned

  • NR or NMN (B3 derivatives): support NAD⁺ pools, relevant for mitochondrial and sirtuin activity.
  • Spermidine: associated with autophagy‑supportive pathways; pairs conceptually with low‑AGE, low‑BCAA days.
  • Urolithin A: supports mitophagy and mitochondrial quality control, especially alongside fasting or low‑AGE meals.

Evening · with light food or as tolerated

Recovery, Sleep & Fasting Support

  • Magnesium (e.g., glycinate): supports relaxation, sleep quality, and muscle function.
  • Additional glycine (if not in GlyNAC): can support sleep architecture and collagen synthesis.
  • On fasting day: prioritize electrolytes (sodium, potassium, magnesium) and skip calorie‑containing supplements.
